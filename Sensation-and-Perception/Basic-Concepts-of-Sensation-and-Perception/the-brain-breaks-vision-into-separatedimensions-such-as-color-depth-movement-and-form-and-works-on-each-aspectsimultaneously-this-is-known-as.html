BASIC CONCEPTS OF SENSATION AND PERCEPTION

[SOURCES]
The brain breaks vision into separatedimensions such as color, depth, movement and form, and works on each aspectsimultaneously. This is known as:

(A) Feature detection

(B) ** Parallel processing

(C) Accommodation

(D) Opponent processing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The brain breaks vision into separate dimensions such as color, depth, movement, and form, and works on each aspect simultaneously. This is called: A. feature detection.

Concept note-2: -The delicious smell of garlic and tomatoes is almost overwhelming when you first walk through the door. You sit down to wait for a table, and after a few minutes, the scents dissipate until you barely notice them. This is an example of sensory adaptation.

Concept note-3: -Other Examples of Sensory Adaptation Hearing–loud sound causes a small muscle attached to one of the bones of the inner ear to contract, reducing the transmission of sound vibrations to the inner ear, where the vibrations are detected.

Concept note-4: -The idea that knowledge comes from inborn ways of organizing sensory experiences was proposed by the philosopher IMMANUEL KANT . 2. On the other side were philosophers who maintained that we learn to perceive the world by experiencing it.

Concept note-5: -How do the eye and brain process visual information? After processing by bipolar and ganglion cells in the eyes’ retina, neural impulses travel through the optic nerve, to the thalamus, and on to the visual cortex. In the visual cortex, feature detectors respond to specific features of the visual stimulus.
