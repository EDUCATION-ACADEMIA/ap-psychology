The apparent convergence of parallel lines as they recede (‘go back’) into the distance

(A) Lunar perspective

(B) Texture gradient

(C) Texture ground

(D) ** Linear perspective

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A vanishing point is a point on the image plane of a perspective rendering where the two-dimensional perspective projections of mutually parallel lines in three-dimensional space appear to converge.

Concept note-2: -Linear perspective is the apparent convergence of parallel lines as they recede into the distance.

Concept note-3: -Linear perspective is a type of monocular cue in which parallel lines appear to converge at some point in the distance.

Concept note-4: -linear perspective, a system of creating an illusion of depth on a flat surface. All parallel lines (orthogonals) in a painting or drawing using this system converge in a single vanishing point on the composition’s horizon line.

Concept note-5: -Linear Perspective. Linear perspective is a depth cue that is related to both relative size and the next depth cue, texture gradient. In linear perspective parallel lines that recede into the distance appear to get closer together or converge.
