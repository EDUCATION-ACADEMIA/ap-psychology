What is job rotation?

(A) ** moving employees between different tasks to promote experience and variety

(B) increasing the scope of a job through extending the range of its job duties and responsibilities

(C) give employees greater responsibility by increasing the range and complexity of tasks

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Job rotation is the practice of moving employees between jobs in an organization. These rotations are predominantly lateral, meaning that they happen between jobs on the same level and are not considered promotions. They are also often temporary with people moving back to their original job after a certain time.

Concept note-2: -Job rotation Job rotation is a practice of moving employees between jobs in an organization. This increases the skill variety, helps employees orientate in potential new roles, and learn through different experiences.

Concept note-3: -Job rotation is the systematic movement of employees from one job to another within the organization to achieve various human resources objectives such as orienting new employees, training employees, enhancing career development, and preventing job boredom or burnout.

Concept note-4: -Job rotations also result in a transfer of sorts, from one department to another, but they are not necessarily meant to be a permanent move. Instead, employees are moved with the expectation that they will remain in that position for a period of time before moving on to the next job in the rotation.

Concept note-5: -The purpose of job rotation includes enabling employees to develop knowledge and skills, understand the company better and effectively utilize the companyâ€™s staff. The two primary types of job rotation are task rotation and position rotation.
