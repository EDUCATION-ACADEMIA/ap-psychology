(A) ** The author’s claim suggests that people have more control over their personal happiness than previously believed.

(D) There is not evidence in the article of previous understandings in comparison to this author’s claims.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How does the author’s claim in this article regarding happiness compare to previous understandings of happiness? The author’s claim suggests that people have more control over their personal happiness than previously believed.

Concept note-2: -The Three Keys to Happiness Scientists have found that the three things that make people most happy are PLEASURE (doing things you enjoy), ENGAGEMENT (feeling interested in your activities and connected to others), and MEANING (feeling like what you do matters).

Concept note-3: -How does the detail about the study of twins contribute to the text (Paragraph 22)? A. It proves that environment has a significant impact on a person’s happiness, as twins do not the same level of happiness after being separated.

Concept note-4: -Happiness can be measured by these three factors: the presence of positive emotions, the absence of negative emotions, and life satisfaction (Ryan & Deci, 2001). It is a uniquely subjective experience, which means that nobody is better at reporting on someone’s happiness than the individuals themselves.
