Just prior to awakening Mohammed from a hypnotic state, the therapist told him that during the next few days he would feel nauseous whenever he reached for a cigarette. Mohammed’s therapist was attempting to make use of

(A) the cocktail party effect

(B) ** posthypnotic suggestion.

(C) hypnagogic sensations

(D) REM rebound

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Taking a different approach to explain hypnosis, the social-cognitive theory of hypnosis sees people in hypnotic states as performing the social role of a hypnotized person. As you will learn when you study social roles, people’s behavior can be shaped by their expectations of how they should act in a given situation.

Concept note-2: -Hypnosis is a changed state of awareness and increased relaxation that allows for improved focus and concentration. It also is called hypnotherapy. Hypnosis usually is done with the guidance of a health care provider using verbal repetition and mental images. During hypnosis, most people feel calm and relaxed.

Concept note-3: -The primary uses of hypnosis and relaxation techniques are in anxiety, in disorders with a strong psychological component (such as asthma and irritable bowel syndrome), and in conditions that can be modulated by levels of arousal (such as pain). They are also commonly used in programmes for stress management.

Concept note-4: -Hypnosis usually includes suggestions for relaxation, calmness, and overall well-being, which may last just during the session but can sometimes be reactivated later by the patient.
