EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Target population refers to the ENTIRE group of individuals or objects to which researchers are interested in generalizing the conclusions. The target population usually has varying characteristics and it is also known as the theoretical population.

Concept note-2: -A(n) census is conducted when a researcher investigates the entire group of people they are interested in. If a studyâ€™s sample is not generalizable to the rest of the population, we would say that the study has external validity that is poor.

Concept note-3: -The target population is the entire population, or group, that a researcher is interested in researching and analysing. A sampling frame is then drawn from this target population.

Concept note-4: -The sample frame is the group of individuals that can be selected from the target population given the sampling process used in the study.
