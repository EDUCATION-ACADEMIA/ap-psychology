What is a random sample?

(A) ** All members of the target population have an equal chance of being selected.

(B) Every nth member of a target population is selected to be in a sample.

(C) The composition of the sample reflects the proportions of people in certain sub-groups (strata) within the target population / wider population.

(D) The researcher selects anyone who happens to be willing and available at the time of their study

(E) Involves participants selecting themselves to be a part of the sample.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In simple random sampling (SRS), each sampling unit of a population has an equal chance of being included in the sample. Consequently, each possible sample also has an equal chance of being selected. To select a simple random sample, you need to list all of the units in the survey population.

Concept note-2: -A simple random sample is a randomly selected subset of a population. In this sampling method, each member of the population has an exactly equal chance of being selected.

Concept note-3: -Random selection refers to how sample members (study participants) are selected from the population for inclusion in the study. Random assignment is an aspect of experimental design in which study participants are assigned to the treatment or control group using a random procedure.

Concept note-4: -Key Takeaways. A simple random sample is one of the methods researchers use to choose a sample from a larger population. This method works if there is an equal chance that any of the subjects in a population will be chosen. Researchers choose simple random sampling to make generalizations about a population.

Concept note-5: -A representative sample is a small subset group that seeks to proportionally reflect specified characteristics exemplified in a target population.
