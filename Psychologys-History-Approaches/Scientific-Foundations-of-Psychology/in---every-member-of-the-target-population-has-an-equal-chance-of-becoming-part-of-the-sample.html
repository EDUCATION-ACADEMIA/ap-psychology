In ____ ____ every member of the target population has an equal chance of becoming part of the sample.

(A) Stratified sampling

(B) ** Random sampling

(C) Opportunity sampling

(D) Self-selected sampling

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Simple random sampling. In simple random sampling (SRS), each sampling unit of a population has an equal chance of being included in the sample. Consequently, each possible sample also has an equal chance of being selected.

Concept note-2: -Random sampling is a type of probability sampling where everyone in the entire target population has an equal chance of being selected.

Concept note-3: -Simple random sampling is the basic sampling technique where we select a group of subjects (a sample) for study from a larger group (a population). Each individual is chosen entirely by chance and each member of the population has an equal chance of being included in the sample.

Concept note-4: -A representative sample is a small subset group that seeks to proportionally reflect specified characteristics exemplified in a target population.
