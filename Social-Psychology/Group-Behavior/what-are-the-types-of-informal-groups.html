What are the types of informal groups?

(A) Functional Teams and Self-Directed Teams

(B) Common and Task Group

(C) ** Interest and Friendship Group

(D) Virtual Teams and Task Forces

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An interest group always involves members sharing common and mutual goals. For example, a firm of partners will be an interest group. A friendship group, however, need not have common interests but members share mutual attributes. For example, they may belong to the same place or support the same sports team.

Concept note-2: -An interest group is usually informal, and is a group of people who band together to attain a specific objective with which each member is concerned. Within an organization, this might be a group of people who come together to demand better working conditions or a better employee evaluation process.

Concept note-3: -There are two types of group, namely, formal groups and informal groups. Formal groups are the ones that are created as per official authority, so as to fulfill the desired objective. Unlike, informal groups are formed by the employees as per their likes, interests, and attitudes.

Concept note-4: -An informal group may be a friendship group, interest group, reference group, or membership group.
